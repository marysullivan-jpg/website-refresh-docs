Internal Linking Best Practices Applied

Topic clusters (hub & spoke)Each pillar page (/advisors, /clients or equivalent, /services) acts as a hub linking down to all child pages. Children link back up to the hub and cross-link to siblings. This concentrates topical authority.
3-click depth maximumEvery page is reachable within 3 clicks from the homepage. The deepest pages (e.g., individual service pages, comparison pages) are 3 levels deep at most.
Cross-audience bridgingBoth options include deliberate "off-ramp" links between the advisor and client sides — the homepage journey paths, the toggle, and contextual CTAs like "Are you an advisor?" / "Looking for a financial advisor?" ensure neither audience hits a dead end.
Contextual links at decision pointsTestimonials and advisor stories are linked from value prop pages, solution pages, and the homepage — placed where prospects need social proof to keep moving through the funnel.
CTA consistencyEvery page has a primary CTA (demo for advisors, find-an-advisor for clients) plus a secondary cross-audience CTA. No orphan pages without a next step.
Breadcrumb navigationEvery child page includes breadcrumb links back to its parent and the homepage, reinforcing hierarchy for both users and search engines.
Footer link equityFooter links to key pages (About, Careers, FAQ, Terms, Privacy, both audience landing pages) provide sitewide link equity to pages that matter for trust and SEO.
Comparison pages (hidden from nav)The /advisors/savvy-vs-* pages are intentionally excluded from navigation but are contextually linked from relevant advisor pages — this keeps nav clean while supporting bottom-of-funnel SEO queries.
